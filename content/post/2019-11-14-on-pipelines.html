Problems in mind

Where do you live?

Code files usually live in one folder, which is also a GitHub folder that you and your team commit/push to. So far, so good. But what do you do with the data to feed that monter pipeline of yours?

I will assume that your concerns with data privacy are minor or you handled them accordingly (only private parties have access to the data).

Now, you still have the problem of where to put this other folder, which is basically a size problem.

Small files can live with your data

This is the case for small and few text files of some thousand rows. Easy enough, you just go with your /repo-name/data/ and live hapily ever after.

Medium size files

These files are big enough to be a problem for hosting on GitHub. File formats start to be an issue here, images and video will not be easily accessible anywhere you take it.

Options:

  • Good old-fashioned external hard drive.

Pros: This is a good one if your data is in the Gb and you don’t really need.

Cons: Hard drives fail. Are you ready to lose your data? It starts to get really annoying when you have to do back-ups of your data and your data is big enough that you can’t use your computer’s hard drive (that’s why you chose an external hard drive in the first place).
